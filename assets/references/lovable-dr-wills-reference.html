Our story

Three different worlds.
One better bottle.

Dr. Will's did not begin with a trend forecast or a boardroom brief. It began with three people looking at everyday food from different angles and arriving at the same question: why could the sauce not be better?

Image placeholder

Three different worlds

Will was a doctor thinking about what families were eating. Josh was a restaurant owner who knew that better food still had to taste brilliant. Liam brought the commercial experience to help turn an idea into something real. Different starting points, but the same belief: everyday sauce could be made better.

Image placeholder

The problem was already on the table

For Will, the question became personal when he noticed how much sugar could be hiding in the ketchup served with children's meals. Josh was seeing the same problem from a restaurant kitchen: carefully considered food was often finished with a sauce that had received far less thought.

Image placeholder

Could ketchup be made better?

The idea sounded simple: make a ketchup from recognisable ingredients, avoid relying on added refined sugar, and make sure it still delivered the proper flavour people expected. In practice, getting that balance right took patience, testing, and a lot of tasting.

Image placeholder

From kitchen table to first bottle

The early versions were made in small batches and refined one bottle at a time. The labels, recipes, and packaging were still taking shape, but the reason for making them was already clear: create an everyday sauce people could understand and genuinely enjoy.

Image placeholder

Out into the real world

Dr. Will's moved from early experiments to markets, shelves, and the hands of real customers. Those first sales showed that people were ready for a sauce made with more thought, as long as better ingredients never meant compromising on taste.

Image placeholder

One bottle became a whole range

What began with a better ketchup grew into a cupboard of sauces for real meals and real life. The range has expanded, but the original idea remains the same: recognisable ingredients, proper flavour, and a better bottle for the food people already love.
